Vision Camera TMC Accuracy: Baseline vs. 1st Calibration

Manual counts vs. Vision Camera turning-movement counts, compared across every intersection with recorded data — Hennepin County Transportation Operations.

Project goal & methodology

Hennepin County's Transportation Operations Department uses a Vision Camera system that automatically generates turning-movement counts (TMC) at signalized intersections — a task traditionally done by a person standing at the intersection with a tally sheet. Before the county can rely on the Vision Camera's counts, it needs to know how accurate they actually are. This project's goal was to answer that question directly: manually count vehicles at a sample of intersections and compare those manual counts against what the Vision Camera recorded for the same time window.

Manual counts were collected in 15-minute windows at each intersection and compared to the Vision Camera's count for that same window, using two standard traffic-engineering accuracy metrics:

  • % Difference — the plain relative error, (Vision − Manual) / Manual. Simple to read, but treats a 2-vehicle error the same whether the intersection carries 10 vehicles or 1,000.
  • GEH — a chi-squared-derived statistic used industry-wide (state DOTs, FHWA) to flag meaningful count discrepancies. It weights the error by count magnitude, so it isn't fooled by a large absolute gap on a high-volume intersection or an alarmed by a small absolute gap on a low-volume one.

Methodology note: these are 15-minute interval counts, not hourly volumes. GEH scales with √(volume) for a fixed relative error, so the conventional GEH < 5 "good match" threshold — calibrated against hourly counts — is not directly applicable to raw 15-minute totals: scaling the same counts to an hourly-equivalent basis (×4 volume) roughly doubles every GEH value. KPI tiles below report both the as-collected GEH and the hourly-equivalent GEH pass rate, rather than applying the threshold to only one basis.

Consultant vs. Vision Camera — a third data source

Separately from the Manual-vs-Vision comparison above, a professional traffic consultant's counts were compared against the Vision Camera's counts at __N_CONSULTANT__ intersections. This is kept as its own comparison rather than folded into the Baseline/1st Calibration numbers, because the counting method is fundamentally different: consultant counts are cumulative totals over long, variable windows (as short as 13 hours, as long as 18) rather than fixed 15-minute windows.

Why GEH runs so high here: GEH scales with √(volume) for a fixed relative error. A 13–18 hour count accumulates thousands of vehicles, so the same (or even a better) % accuracy as the 15-minute Manual counts produces a much larger GEH — this is a mechanical effect of count volume, not evidence the Vision Camera is less accurate at these intersections. The conventional GEH<5 threshold was calibrated for hourly volumes, so it's shown here on two bases: as-collected over the full multi-hour window, and normalized to an hourly rate (each total divided by its own count duration before computing GEH) — the latter is the fairer comparison against GEH<5. Even so, % Difference remains the more directly meaningful metric for this dataset, since it doesn't depend on count duration at all.
